Description

Djent (/dʒɛnt/) (also known as Djent metal) is a subgenre of progressive metal.
Its distinctive sound is a high-gain, distorted, palm-muted, low-pitch guitar sound. The name "Djent" is an onomatopoeia of this sound.

Source: Wikipedia

Origins

Djent is first heard on Meshuggah's second album Destroy Erase Improve in 1995. But how did they come up with the name and sound?
Mårten Hagström, a band member, shared the following story:

"First of all, we're very sorry for creating that genre. We didn't intend to — our bad. I think it's a misconception, that djent thing."
"It was our lead guitar player, Fredrik, being drunk back in the day, talking to one of our old-school fans, trying to explain what type of guitar tone we were always trying to get, and he was desperately trying to say:
'We want that 'dj—' 'dj—,' 'dj—,' 'dj—,' Hagström explained, imitating a riff. "And that guy was, like, 'What's he saying? Is that a Swedish word? Must be. Sounds like dj_, maybe 'djent'? Maybe something like that.'"

"And that's where it comes from: a drunk misunderstanding, as always, with Meshuggah."

Source: Revolvermag.com
